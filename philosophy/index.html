Build Philosophy

Calm technical confidence, practical systems design, and execution quality over performative complexity.

Principles

  1. Design for reality: account for operator behavior, edge cases, and failure points from the start.
  2. Favor compounding systems: build tools and workflows that keep paying dividends.
  3. Keep architecture legible: future maintainers should understand why choices were made.
  4. Measure value in outcomes: reliability, speed, and decision quality matter more than novelty.
